Our Story

A Journey of Passion, Flavor & Tradition

2010

The Dream Begins

Ahmed Khan, a young culinary enthusiast from Gulshan-e-Iqbal, had a dream. Growing up, he watched his grandmother prepare the most delicious pakoras every evening. The aroma, the crispy texture, the perfect blend of spices - it was magic.

After years of working in various restaurants across Karachi, Ahmed decided it was time to share his grandmother's secret recipe with the world. With just ‚Ç®50,000 in savings, a single fryer, and an unshakeable belief in his dream, Pakora Palace was born in a small 200 sq ft shop.

2012

Word Spreads

Within two years, Pakora Palace became the talk of the neighborhood. People would line up every evening, waiting for their turn to taste the legendary pakoras. Ahmed's commitment to quality - using only fresh vegetables, premium gram flour, and his grandmother's secret spice blend - set him apart.

The shop expanded to accommodate more customers, and Ahmed hired his first two employees. The family recipe was now feeding hundreds of families across Karachi.

2015

Expanding the Menu

Ahmed began experimenting with new varieties. Chicken pakora, paneer pakora, and the now-famous Mix Pakora Platter were introduced. Each recipe was perfected over months of testing, ensuring it met the high standards set by the original vegetable pakora.

The team grew to 8 members, and delivery service was launched. Pakora Palace was no longer just a shop - it was becoming a brand.

2018

Award-Winning Excellence

Pakora Palace was featured in "Karachi's Best Street Food" by Dawn News and won the "People's Choice Award" for Best Pakoras in Karachi. Ahmed's dedication to maintaining quality while scaling the business was recognized across the city.

Food bloggers, celebrities, and food critics started visiting. The small shop from Gulshan-e-Iqbal was now a culinary landmark.

2020

Overcoming Adversity

The pandemic hit hard. Like many businesses, Pakora Palace faced unprecedented challenges. But Ahmed's response was swift - he pivoted to contactless delivery, introduced family combo packs, and launched an online ordering system.

During lockdown, Pakora Palace provided free meals to healthcare workers and distributed food to those in need. The community that had supported them for years was now being supported back.

2023

Going Digital

Pakora Palace launched its official website and mobile app, making it easier than ever for customers to order their favorite pakoras. The business embraced technology while staying true to its traditional roots.

Online orders now account for 60% of sales, and the customer base has expanded beyond Karachi, with people ordering for their loved ones across the city.

2025 - TODAY

The Journey Continues

Today, Pakora Palace serves over 500 customers daily, employs 25 team members, and has become a household name in Karachi. But the mission remains the same - to serve the crispiest, most flavorful pakoras made with love and tradition.

Ahmed still personally oversees the spice blending every morning, ensuring every batch meets his grandmother's standards. Because at Pakora Palace, it's not just about food - it's about preserving memories, creating moments, and bringing families together.

Bilal's Journey: From Dishwasher to Master Chef

Master Chef at Pakora Palace

Bilal Sheikh joined Pakora Palace in 2011 as a dishwasher. He was just 18 years old, fresh from his village in Punjab, with dreams of making it in the big city. Ahmed noticed Bilal's curiosity - he would always watch the chefs work, asking questions during breaks.

"I saw hunger in his eyes," Ahmed recalls. "Not for food, but for knowledge." Ahmed started teaching Bilal the basics - how to cut vegetables, how to mix the batter, the importance of temperature control. Bilal was a quick learner.

Today, Bilal is our Master Chef, responsible for training new team members and maintaining our quality standards. He's perfected the art of the spice blend and can tell if a pakora is ready just by the sound of the sizzle.

"Ahmed bhai didn't just give me a job, he gave me a future. Now I'm sending my younger brother to college with what I earn here. This is my family." - Bilal Sheikh

The Wedding Day Miracle

A Story of Going Above & Beyond

It was December 2019. Ayesha was getting married in two days, and her caterer had just canceled. In a panic, she called Pakora Palace at 11 PM, crying. She needed pakoras for 300 guests - her late mother's favorite food, and she wanted to honor her memory.

Ahmed and his team worked through the night. They sourced extra ingredients, brought in additional staff, and prepared everything fresh on the wedding day. They even created a special "Mother's Love" platter with Ayesha's mother's favorite varieties.

When Ayesha tasted the pakoras, she broke down in tears. "It tastes exactly like my mother used to make them," she said. The entire wedding party praised the food, but for Ahmed, the real reward was seeing Ayesha smile on her special day.

"We don't just serve food, we serve emotions. That night, we served love and memories." - Ahmed Khan

Fatima's Rise: Breaking Barriers

Operations Manager

In 2016, Fatima Ali walked into Pakora Palace asking for a job. As a single mother of two, she needed work desperately. But she had no experience in the food industry. Ahmed saw something special in her determination and organizational skills.

Starting as a customer service representative, Fatima revolutionized how orders were managed. She introduced a digital tracking system, improved delivery routes, and created a customer feedback loop that helped improve service quality.

Within three years, she became Operations Manager. Under her leadership, customer satisfaction increased by 40%, and delivery times improved by 25%. She's also mentored five other women who now work at Pakora Palace.

"Ahmed bhai believed in me when no one else did. Now I want to be that person for other women who need a chance." - Fatima Ali

When Karachi Flooded: A Community Comes Together

August 2020

The monsoon rains of August 2020 devastated parts of Karachi. Thousands were displaced, homes were destroyed, and people were hungry. Pakora Palace's own shop was flooded with two feet of water.

Instead of closing down to clean up, Ahmed made a decision: "We'll clean later. People need food now." The team set up temporary cooking stations on higher ground and started preparing meals. For three weeks, Pakora Palace served free food to flood victims.

The community rallied. Regular customers donated money and ingredients. Volunteers helped with cooking and distribution. Together, they served over 10,000 meals. When asked about the financial loss, Ahmed simply said, "Money comes and goes. But humanity? That's forever."

The story went viral on social media. News channels covered it. But for Ahmed and his team, the real reward was seeing families eat hot meals during their darkest hours.

"Karachi gave us everything. When Karachi needed us, how could we not give back?" - Ahmed Khan
